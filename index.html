HEART ATTACK RISK PREDICTION DATASET ANALYSIS

Performed an in-depth analysis of heart attack risk factors using statistical techniques.

Situation:

Healthcare professionals needed a data-driven approach to understand key factors influencing heart attack risks.

Task:

Analyze heart disease data using statistical methods to uncover significant risk factors and build a predictive model.

Actions:

  • Conducted descriptive analysis to summarize patient data
  • Performed ANOVA and Chi-Square tests, identifying a significant association between exercise-induced angina and heart attack likelihood (**p < 0.001**).
  • Applied simple and multiple linear regression to analyze relationships between **age, resting blood pressure, and cholesterol with heart attack risks.
  • Developed a logistic regression model to predict heart attack probability based on chest pain type, sex, and age.

Results:

Discovered a strong association between chest pain type and heart attack risk. The logistic regression model achieved an AUC score of **0.822**, demonstrating high predictive accuracy.

Tools & Techniques Used

  • Python: Pandas, NumPy, SciPy for statistical analysis
  • Excel: Used to validate statistical results
  • Statistical Methods: ANOVA, Chi-Square Test, Regression Analysis, Logistic Regression
